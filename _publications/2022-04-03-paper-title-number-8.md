This paper explores the applications of quantum computing in computational chemistry, demonstrating potential advantages over classical methods.

**Abstract**

Quantum computing holds tremendous promise for computational chemistry, offering the potential to simulate molecular systems with unprecedented accuracy. In this paper, we review current quantum algorithms for chemistry applications and present new approaches for solving electronic structure problems. Our demonstrations on quantum hardware and simulators show quantum advantage for specific chemical systems, paving the way for future applications in drug discovery and materials science.
