**Abstract**

Understanding how large language models (LLMs) organize and represent multiple topics internally remains a challenge. In this paper, we introduce the concept of "topic latent walls" to describe the boundaries that LLMs create between different topics in their latent space. We provide a comprehensive analysis of these walls, demonstrating their existence and exploring their properties. Our findings suggest that topic latent walls play a crucial role in how LLMs manage and generate coherent text across diverse subjects.
