This paper presents a methodology for characterizing exoplanet atmospheres using transmission spectroscopy techniques.

**Abstract**

Transmission spectroscopy provides a powerful tool for studying the atmospheres of exoplanets by analyzing starlight filtered through planetary atmospheres during transits. In this study, we present a comprehensive methodology for extracting and interpreting atmospheric features from transmission spectra, applying our approach to several hot Jupiter systems. Our results demonstrate the ability to identify molecular species and constrain atmospheric properties, contributing to our understanding of exoplanet composition and formation.
