**Abstract**

In this work, we investigate hallucination in large language models from an inner representation perspective. We find that the inner representations of LLMs contain signals that can be used to detect and mitigate hallucinations. Specifically, we introduce the concept of in-context sharpness, which measures the concentration of inner representations, and show that it can serve as an effective alert signal for hallucination.

Our approach provides a new perspective for hallucination mitigation and achieves strong performance on various benchmarks.
