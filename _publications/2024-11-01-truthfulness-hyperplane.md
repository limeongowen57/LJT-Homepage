**Abstract**

In this paper, we explore the inner mechanisms of large language models and discover a universal truthfulness hyperplane that governs truthfulness across diverse tasks. We show that the internal representations of truthful and untruthful outputs can be separated by a hyperplane, and this hyperplane transfers across different models and tasks.

Our findings provide new insights into the interpretability of LLMs and suggest potential approaches for improving model truthfulness.
